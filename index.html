About Me

Results-Oriented Problem Solver with Diverse Skills and Experiences

Leveraging my background in counseling, policy analysis, and training and evaluation, I excel at understanding complex issues, synthesizing a variety of data, and developing actionable solutions.

In early 2024, I pivoted to focusing on analytics after working in education and nonprofit roles (which always included some level of data analysis) for over a decade. I've always enjoyed logic and problem-solving and draw from my past experience to communicate complex messages to a variety of stakeholders.

I look forward to seeing how we can work together.

Relevant Work

Edgewood College

Academic and Career Counselor • August 2016 - August 2019

I supported the academic success and career development of approximately 120 students in the College Completion program. This included synthesizing student enrollment data and academic plans to assist the Registrar's Office in making decisions about future course offerings, developing and implementing a new online orientation using Blackboard LMS, tracking student engagement data through Blackboard Analytics, and assisting in updating and revising policy language through participation in the college-wide Adult Student Learning Committee.

Evident Change

Program Associate • January 2014 - February 2015

I translated complex statutory language, state policies, and case review data into actionable recommendations for departments of human services, developed, implemented, and conducted statewide and nationwide trainings and workgroups of evidence-based assessments and family engagement practices, pioneered the use of pre-implementation reviews to identify potential risks and opportunities and to establish baseline indicators for transforming human services systems, and authored blogs, literature reviews, and e-learning modules for public audiences.

Wisconsin Center for Education Research

Policy and Research Analyst • June 2012 - December 2013

I co-authored a key policy memo outlining options for the Wisconsin Department of Public Instruction’s transition to Smarter Balanced assessments and use of value-added measures, developed and delivered in-person trainings on student achievement measures to technical audiences, practitioners, and administrators, served as Project Manager for the Hillsborough County Public Schools Value-Added Project (7 years, $1.7 million per year), provided technical assistance regarding teacher evaluation measures to departments of education and charter school organizations as part of the Teacher Incentive Fund (TIF), and co-lead an initiative to update and redesign Center’s website.

Education Pioneers

Fellow • June 2012 - December 2013

I synthesized census data, MCAS scores, and obesity data using ArcGIS to create a site suitability index and kernel density map to identify market opportunities in Massachusetts; the organization subsequently expanded to Lawrence—a recommended market, created and presented a six-month strategic plan to board members and key community stakeholders for the launch of Playworks Training, and worked with the marketing department to create targeted one-pagers for prospective clients and fundraising materials (e.g., brochures and web articles).
